Sushi

There is a reason the Japanese have a mean age much higher than those of other countries: their diets are very healthy! A staple food of the Japanese culture is sushi. Sushi comes in many forms, but at its base it is sticky white rice packaged closely with raw fish, such as salmon or tuna. Not all sushi has fish though, some have egg, eel or other base ingredients. Nevertheless,much of the sushi you might eat has fish, rice, and often seaweed. Many dishes come with vegetables, different sauces, etc.

Ingredients Needed:

  • Fresh, sticky white rice.
  • Fish, or other substitute.
  • Seaweed.
  • Vegetables (cucumbers, avacado, celery, etc.)

Steps:

  1. Lay cooked rice into sushi roller and evenly spread.
  2. At the center of the rice square, lay down your strips of fish or fish alternative.
  3. Lay down the vegetables and other ingredients immediately adjacent to the fish.
  4. Roll the sushi in the roller until tightly packed.
  5. Seaweed can be placed on outside of roll, inside, or in the middle of the roll.
  6. ***Some sushi is not assembled as a roll, rather, the fis his placed directly onto bar of rice often with wasabi (Sashimi)
